' The key takeaway from the programme was how to stand out and be abnormal. '


" I’m Yvonne Wamuyu Gichungu, and I was in the 2nd cohort of the Nashon Okowa mentorship programme from June 2022 to December 2022. I must say that was a very significant period in my student life because I transitioned to an abnormality. The opportunity to meet my role model, whom I’d mostly see on LinkedIn, and get to learn so much from him was amazing.

Mr. Nashon was and is still very passionate about mentorship, and he would always make time for our sessions. The mentorship was not just about being a good construction manager. It was more about personal development and being the best version of yourself in everything you do in life.

The key takeaway from the programme was how to stand out and "be abnormal". Being in the programme not only provided an opportunity for me to learn but also to improve my communication skills, be self-aware, and make useful connections. From the programme, I learned to appreciate the importance of being disciplined and always doing my best. After the programme, I’m glad to say I got to start my own business and take up some leadership roles in different forums. My fellow mentees were accountability partners, and we have been doing great things together to date.

I’d highly recommend the programme to any student who wants to get out of their comfort zone and be abnormal. "

Yvonne Wamuyu Gichungu,
Mentee
